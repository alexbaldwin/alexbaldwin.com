Inter

A typeface carefully crafted for computer screens

Why Typography Matters

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing. This specimen demonstrates flawless typography using Inter—a variable font designed specifically for computer screens.

The Craft of Type

“Typography is two-dimensional architecture, based on experience and imagination, and guided by rules and readability,” said Hermann Zapf. This quote—from one of history’s greatest type designers—captures the balance between creativity and function.

Line Length and Rhythm

When setting body text, the optimal line length is 45–75 characters. Lines that are too long cause readers to lose their place; lines too short break the reading rhythm.

The screen measures 6′ 2″ diagonally. That’s 6 feet, 2 inches using proper prime symbols.
